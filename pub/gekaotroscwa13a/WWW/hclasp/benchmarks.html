Experiments with static heuristic modifications

We report on a systematic study comparing single heuristic modifications. We focus on well-known ASP planning benchmarks in order to contrast heuristic modifications on comparable problems: Labyrinth, Sokoban, and Hanoi Tower, each comprising 32 instances from the third ASP competition. We contrast the aforementioned baseconfiguration with 38 heuristic modifications, (separately) promoting the choice of actions and fluents via the heuristic modifiers factor (1,2,4,8,16), init (2,4,8,16), level (1,-1), sign (1,-1), as well as attributing values to factor, init, and level by ascending and descending time points. All instances are satisfiable except for one third in Sokoban.

Experiments on abductive problems with optimization

We report experiments on three different problems using abduction in combination with a minimize statement to minimize the number of abducibles. We consider benchmarks of Circuit Diagnosis, Metabolic Network Expansion and Transcriptional Network Repair. To supporting minimization, we assign false to the abducibles (sign=-1) and gradually increase the bias of their choice by imposing factor 2, 4, 8 or 16, or we enforce it via a level modifier (level=1).

Experiments on PDDL planning

We report experiments on PDDL planning. We selected 20 instances from the STRIPS domains of the 2000 and 2002 planning competitions. In turn, we translated these PDDL instances into facts via plasp and used a simple planning encoding with 15 different plan lengths (5, 10, ..., 75). We devised a dynamic heuristic that aims at propagating fluents' truth values backwards in time. Attributing levels via l-T+1 aims at proceeding depth-first from the goal fluents:

       _heuristic(holds(F,T-1),true, lasttime-T+1) :- holds(F,T).
       _heuristic(holds(F,T-1),false,lasttime-T+1) :- not holds(F,T), fluent(F), time(T).
